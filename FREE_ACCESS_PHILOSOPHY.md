## 🤝 How This Works Sustainably

### The MIT License

SSDB uses the **MIT License**, which:
- Permits commercial use (we won't stop you)
- Requires attribution (gives credit)
- Disclaims liability (protects maintainers)
- Allows modification (enables improvement)

**Note**: While the license permits commercial use, we explicitly choose not to commercialize it ourselves and encourage others to keep it freely accessible.

### Sustainable Through Contribution

Instead of payment, we sustain through:

**Community Contributions**:
- Code improvements
- Bug reports
- Documentation enhancements
- Testing and validation
- Translation to other languages
- Tutorial creation

**Academic Collaboration**:
- Research partnerships
- Student projects
- Academic citations
- Conference presentations
- Joint publications

**Organizational Support** (Optional, never required):
- Institutional hosting of repositories
- Compute resources for testing
- Documentation platforms
- Community forum hosting

However:
- The original will always be free

- Community can choose free versions
- Paid versions must compete with free alternatives
- We'll clearly mark the official free version

**We trust**:
- Academic integrity will prefer free access
- Researchers will cite the original
- Ethical organizations will choose freely-given tools
- Community will support open alternatives
